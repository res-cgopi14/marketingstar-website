Promotional SMS campaigns: Crafting messages that drive instant action

Jules Sen Wang

February 9, 2025

Promotional SMS campaigns are a powerful way to connect with customers and encourage instant action. Text messages boast open rates nearing 98% within minutes and response rates as high as 45%, making SMS ideal for delivering time-sensitive promotions and personalized offers.

What makes promotional SMS campaigns special isn’t just the channel’s effectiveness, but the strategy of delivering tailored, compelling messages that speak directly to the customer’s needs. In this blog, we’ll break down the key components of an effective promotional SMS that converts and show you how Marketing Star can help you achieve your marketing goals.

Why promotional SMS campaigns work so well

SMS campaigns are effective because they are direct and immediate. SMS notifications pop up directly on mobile screens, creating instant visibility. When paired with good timing and concise messaging, these communications are tailored made for boosting flash sales, limited-time offers, and personalized deals. For example, imagine a gym running a discounted membership campaign aimed at inactive members. Sending a personalized promotional SMS can reignite interest and get them back through the door with minimal effort.

The key components of the perfect promotional SMS

  • A recognizable sender name to build trust
    Your SMS campaign’s effectiveness starts with trust. Recipients need to know who’s contacting them to engage confidently. Marketing Star allows you to use branded sender IDs, so recipients instantly recognize your brand.
    For example, instead of an unknown number, your promotional message could display “FitZone Gym” as the sender. This simple branding tactic builds trust and reduces the chance of your message being dismissed.
  • An attention-grabbing opening line
    The first few words of your SMS are crucial. They need to capture attention immediately and convey the offer clearly. Make it personal to capture your audience’s attention. Consider this example for a gym: “Hi Alex! Ready to get back in shape? Join today and get your first month free. Offer expires soon!”
     
    Marketing Star helps you automate personalized opening lines by pulling details like first names, recent activity, or preferences directly into your messages. This customization drives higher open rates and engagement.
  • Concise, action-oriented body text
    Promotional SMS messages should be short and to the point. You only have 160 characters to convey value and encourage action, so every word counts. Clearly state what’s in it for the recipient and include a strong call to action (CTA).
     
    Bad example: “Our gym has promotions available. Visit us if you’re interested.”
     
    Good example: “Sign up this week and get 50% off your first 3 months at FitZone Gym! Don’t miss out. [Join Now].”
     
    Marketing Star’s built-in templates provide pre-designed SMS structures that can be tailored to your offers, ensuring that your message is concise, engaging, and effective.
  • Emojis to enhance the message tone (when appropriate)
    Adding emojis can boost engagement and give your message a friendly, inviting tone. If your brand personality allows for it, use emojis sparingly to convey excitement or urgency.
     
    Example: “💪 Limited-time offer! Get your gym membership today and save 50%. Don’t miss out! [Sign Up].”
  • Clear CTAs with clickable links
    Every promotional SMS should include a clear, actionable CTA to guide the recipient. Whether it’s to “Shop Now,” “Join Today,” or “Claim Your Discount,” the CTA should be prominent and easy to act upon. Hyperlink key actions to make the conversion path seamless.
     
    Example: “Join FitZone Gym now and get 50% off your membership! [Join Now]”

Sample SMS campaign

Here’s how a gym can create a winning SMS campaign:

  • Sender: FitZone Gym
  • Message: Hi Mia! 🏋️ Get back to your fitness goals with 50% off your first 3 months. Join FitZone Gym today and see the results. Offer expires soon! [Join Now]

This message is personalized and points to a clear action the customer can perform in exchange for a benefit. The emoji adds a fun, motivational tone while reinforcing the fitness theme.

Tips for optimizing your SMS campaigns

  • Segment your audience for better targeting
    Use customer data to create segments based on activity level, purchase history, or preferences. Send personalized offers to each segment to improve relevance.
  • Test subject lines and offers
    A/B test different opening lines, CTAs, and promotional offers to see what drives the most engagement.
  • Monitor performance and adjust
    Use Marketing Star’s analytics to track key metrics like open rates, click-through rates, and conversions. Continuously optimize based on performance.
