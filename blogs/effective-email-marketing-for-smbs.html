Effective email marketing for SMBs: Craft subject lines and CTAs that convert

Sneha Subramanian

February 9, 2025

Email marketing remains one of the most powerful tools for small and medium-sized businesses (SMBs) to connect with their audience and drive conversions. But how can you ensure your email marketing efforts succeed? The secret often lies in two crucial elements: subject lines and Calls to Action (CTAs). These elements can either make or break your campaign.

In this blog, we’ll explore how you can craft email subject lines and CTAs that convert—whether you’re focused on B2B email marketing or B2C email marketing.

Why subject lines matter for email marketing success

The subject line is the first thing your recipients see when your email lands in their inbox. In fact, studies show that 33% of email recipients decide whether to open an email based purely on the subject line. This means crafting compelling subject lines is essential to the success of your email marketing campaigns.

Here are some email marketing subject line best practices:

  • Keep it short and sweet
    A concise, to-the-point subject line is indispensible in email marketing for SMBs. Aim for 6-10 words, or 40-60 characters, to ensure your subject line is readable on both desktop and mobile devices.
  • Create a sense of urgency
    Words like “limited time,” “exclusive,” and “act now” are powerful in email marketing subject lines because they encourage the reader to take immediate action.
  • Personalize it
    Personalization is a great way to make your emails stand out. Including the recipient’s name or referring to their past behavior increases engagement and can improve open rates.
  • Use actionable language
    Start your subject line with action verbs like “discover,” “get,” or “unlock” to compel the reader to open your email.
  • Highlight the benefit
    Always focus on the value that your recipient will gain. For example, instead of just saying “Check out our new product,” say “Save 20% on our latest product today!”

Avoid these words to prevent landing in the junk folder

While crafting compelling subject lines is key, you also need to consider spam triggers. Certain words and phrases can cause your email to be flagged by spam filters, increasing the chances of it landing in the junk folder. Here are some common words to avoid:

  • Free (unless contextually appropriate)
  • Guarantee (can trigger suspicion)
  • Urgent (may seem like a scam)
  • Cash or Earn money (commonly associated with spammy emails)
  • Limited time offer (if overused)
  • Risk-free (can raise red flags)
  • Act now or Don’t miss out (too aggressive)
  • Congratulations (may look like a phishing attempt)

These words can trigger spam filters, and using them too frequently can hurt your email deliverability. Always focus on crafting subject lines that are clear, honest, and reflect your brand’s voice.

Crafting CTAs that convert

Once you’ve successfully piqued the recipient’s interest with a compelling subject line, the next step is to get them to take action. This is where your CTA comes into play. A well-crafted CTA matters because it guides the recipient towards your conversion goal.

Here are some tips to make your CTAs more effective:

  • Be clear and specific
    The CTA should clearly state the action you want your recipient to take. Use action words like “Shop Now,” “Sign Up,” or “Learn More.” Avoid vague CTAs like “Click Here.”
  • Create urgency, again
    Like subject lines, CTAs should create a sense of urgency. Phrases such as “Get Your Discount Today” or “Claim Your Free Trial” can encourage recipients to act fast.
  • Use contrasting colors
    Make sure your CTA button stands out by using a contrasting color. This will draw attention and improve click-through rates, a key email marketing metric.
  • Make it easy to take action
    Don’t complicate the process. The simpler the action, the higher the conversion rate. If you’re asking someone to make a purchase, make sure the checkout process is straightforward.
  • Test and optimize
    Testing different versions of CTAs is important. A/B test different CTA buttons, copies, and placements to see what combination resonates best with your audience.

B2B vs. B2C email marketing: Tailor your approach

While the principles of crafting subject lines and CTAs remain the same, your approach may differ depending on whether you’re targeting a B2B or B2C audience. The two segments require different strategies.

B2B email marketing

For SMBs in the B2B space, the focus should be on offering value and addressing specific business needs. It tends to be more formal, professional, and solution-oriented.

Here are some best practices for B2B subject lines and CTAs:

  • Subject line
    Use language like “Increase Your ROI,” “Maximize Efficiency,” or “Solve Your [Industry] Challenges.”
  • CTA:
    Focus on offering value-driven actions such as “Request a Demo,” “Download Our Whitepaper,” or “Start Your Free Trial.”

B2C email marketing

B2C email marketing often focuses on creating a personal connection and triggering immediate action. The tone is more conversational and designed to evoke emotions.

Here are some tips for B2C subject lines and CTAs:

  • Subject line
    Create excitement with phrases like “Shop Our New Collection,” “Limited-Time Offer,” or “Don’t Miss Out on 50% Off!”
  • CTA:
    Encourage fast actions like “Buy Now,” “Claim Your Discount,” or “Sign Up Today.”

Subject lines and CTAs can make or break your email campaigns. By focusing on the value, clarity, and urgency of your messaging, you can create emails that not only just land in your recipient’s inbox but also inspire them to take action.
